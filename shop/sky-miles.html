Sky Miles

by JFeelgood

Acrylic, latex, epoxy resin, Chrome airplane model

"Navigating emotions is to know the water from the winds."
Artist Note

The wind crawls across my flesh, carrying smells from faraway people, a faraway life and society, busy and bustling about. What am I that I cannot find myself with them? I was with them…. but not anymore… do they even know I’m missing? I run my fingers across the scars of my past and remember how they befell me… or do I? I don’t know and it doesn’t matter. I just lie back and enjoy the breeze. Every thought leads to another. Every thought is me overcoming whatever I was before and becoming something more. I am constantly identifying myself with the world around me in some way or another and each new thought aids me in this process. I cannot help but still feel the sadness. I don’t want to be alone but…. who could possibly keep me company? Who could possibly understand the painstaking process of this individualistic evolution of the spirit, this one that is just for me? I sometimes wonder if the hole in my heart is there to remind me not of all that I yearn to fill it with and that it cannot truly be filled, but a reminder to always be open to new things, thoughts, and ideas. Onwards!

The Original Painting

The original Sky Miles is Acrylic, latex, epoxy resin, Chrome airplane model, 24x36, painted in 2017. It currently in artist's studio and is not for sale.

Process Notes

I found a chrome airplane model at a store and thought wouldn't it be lovely to see this as the idea of it in motion? The blur of terrain beneath it as it flies overhead... This was a complex process of pours and waiting, pours and waiting, mounting the plane securely to a board. I felt it was exciting to have the paint going over a frame so it would be undeniable in passing that it was a painting.

Why This Works at Card Scale

Sculptural dimensionality meets fluid abstraction in this aviation-inspired mixed-media triumph. The golden vintage aircraft emerges from swirling turquoise and navy marble textures, creating tactile depth and nostalgic motion that elevates it beyond typical wall art into a statement piece worthy of premium placement.
